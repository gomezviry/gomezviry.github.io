## Common questions

### Where are my notes stored?

On your device, in an encrypted local database. The encryption key lives in
your operating system's secure keystore (Keychain, Keystore, Secret Service,
or Credential Manager, depending on the platform). Moop has no server.

### How does cloud sync work?

Cloud sync is optional and off by default. When you turn it on and connect a
provider, Moop uploads your notes to **your own** account with that provider.
Moop never receives or stores your notes.

### Which providers are supported for sync?

Dropbox, Google Drive, and Microsoft OneDrive. You sign in with your existing
account; Moop only requests permission to read and write its own folder.

### How do I unlock protected or hidden notes?

Use the biometric or system-password prompt your device shows when you open a
protected note. Moop receives only a yes/no result — your biometric data
never leaves the operating system.

### How do I delete my data?

Delete individual notes from inside the app, or uninstall Moop to remove the
local encrypted database and its key. If cloud sync was enabled, deletions
propagate to your provider on the next sync; any remaining files in your
provider account can be removed directly through that provider.

### Can I export my notes?

Yes — notes can be exported as Markdown or PDF from the editor menu.
